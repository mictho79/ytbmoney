Why Cricket Channels Earn Very Low RPM

India dominates cricket viewership

India alone represents 60%+ of global cricket views on YouTube. Google Ads India sports CPMs average $0.50–$1.50. Even a channel with 100 million monthly views will earn a small fraction of what a 10-million-view US channel earns.

Sponsorships dwarf AdSense

Successful cricket channels rely almost entirely on sponsorships from fantasy sports apps (Dream11, MPL, MyTeam11), betting sites (where legal), and direct brand deals. A 1M-view cricket channel might earn $700 in AdSense but $5,000–$15,000 in sponsorships.

Copyright is a minefield

Star India, Sony Pictures Networks, and national cricket boards aggressively claim match footage. Most highlight channels are either Content-ID claimed (losing 50–100% of ad revenue) or operate on the edge of fair use with commentary overlays.

FAQ — Cricket YouTube Earnings

Why is cricket RPM so low compared to football?

Cricket audiences are 85% India/Pakistan/Bangladesh — the three lowest-CPM sports markets globally. Football at least has Brazilian, Mexican, and European viewership pulling averages up. Cricket has no equivalent tier-1 audience base.

Can a cricket channel become profitable on AdSense alone?

Only at massive scale (50M+ monthly views). Most profitable cricket creators treat AdSense as a small secondary revenue source and build their business on sponsorships, affiliate fantasy-app signups, and brand partnerships.

Do UK / Australia cricket channels earn more?

Yes — significantly. A UK-focused cricket channel can reach $2–$4 RPM because Australia and the UK are tier-1 CPM markets. The tradeoff is smaller audience size compared to India-focused content.

What about IPL-focused content?

IPL content has high view volume but the same audience-geography problem. IPL-focused channels see seasonal RPM spikes during March-May when advertiser demand peaks in India.
