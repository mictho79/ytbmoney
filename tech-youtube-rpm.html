Tech YouTube RPM Overview ($5–$12 Average)

Tech YouTube channels earn an average RPM of $5–$12, placing them firmly in the upper tier of YouTube niches. The broad tech category covers a wide range of sub-niches, each with its own advertiser demand profile and CPM range.

Tech content benefits from two key advertiser dynamics: electronics manufacturers (Samsung, Apple, Intel, NVIDIA) advertising products to purchase-intent audiences, and software companies (both consumer and B2B) targeting technical users with high potential customer lifetime value. The result is sustained premium CPM across most tech sub-niches.

Why Tech Has Strong Advertiser Demand

Several factors drive consistently strong CPMs in the tech YouTube space:

Purchase intent is extremely high. Someone watching a "best laptop under $1,000" video is researching an imminent purchase. Electronics retailers, manufacturers, and comparison platforms all bid aggressively for this high-intent audience. Conversion rates are measurable and strong, justifying premium CPM bids.

Software companies have enormous customer LTV. An enterprise software company that pays $15 CPM to reach a developer who then adopts their tool can generate $10,000+ in annual subscription revenue from one converted viewer. The CPM-to-conversion economics are excellent, driving competitive auction dynamics.

AI content boom in 2025–2026. The explosion of AI tool tutorials has attracted a new wave of premium advertisers — AI SaaS companies, productivity platforms, and developer tools — competing for audiences researching AI workflows. AI tutorial content now commands some of the highest tech CPMs at $10–$20+.

Audience demographic. Tech YouTube attracts professionals, developers, students, and enthusiasts with disposable income and active purchasing behavior. This demographic is highly attractive to advertisers and commands premium rates.

How to Maximize RPM in the Tech Niche

Tech YouTubers can push their RPM toward the higher end of the range with these strategies:

Focus on B2B and software tutorials. Consumer tech reviews earn solid RPMs, but software tutorials (especially for enterprise tools, coding frameworks, or AI platforms) attract B2B advertisers paying $12–$20+ CPM. A tutorial for Salesforce, HubSpot, or a popular coding language can command enterprise software advertising rates.

Create "best X for budget" content. Product comparison content ("best laptop under $1,000," "best GPU for 1080p gaming") targets buyers at the decision stage. This purchase intent signal drives the highest CPM bids from electronics advertisers.

Target US-based professional audiences. US developers and IT professionals earn more and spend more on tech tools. Content specifically targeting US audience concerns (US pricing, US availability) attracts the highest-CPM US tech advertisers.

Cover AI tools aggressively. AI tool tutorials currently command the highest CPMs in the tech category. Building a library of tutorials for popular AI tools (productivity apps, coding assistants, design tools) positions you for the segment with the most aggressive advertiser spend.
