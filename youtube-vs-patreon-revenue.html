YouTube AdSense vs Patreon: Which Pays More for Creators?

The answer depends on your niche, how engaged your audience is, and what you're willing to offer — here's the math to decide.

How Each Platform Works

YouTube AdSense pays you based on how many monetized views your videos receive. Advertisers bid on your content, and you receive roughly 55% of ad revenue (YouTube keeps 45%). Your RPM (Revenue Per Mille) reflects your actual earnings per 1,000 views after YouTube's cut. RPM varies widely by niche, seasonality, and audience geography — typically ranging from $1 to $20+.

Patreon is a subscription platform where your audience pays you a fixed monthly amount (e.g., $3, $5, $10) in exchange for exclusive content, early access, or community perks. Patreon charges creators a platform fee of 5–12% depending on the plan tier, plus payment processing fees of around 2–3%.

The key difference: AdSense is passive and scales with views, while Patreon is active and scales with audience loyalty — not raw view count.

Typical Patreon Conversion Rates

Patreon conversion — the percentage of your YouTube subscribers who become paying patrons — typically falls between 1% and 5% for most channels. Highly engaged niche communities (true crime, independent journalism, tabletop gaming, educational content) can occasionally reach 5–10%, but those are outliers.

Here's what conversion looks like in practice:

  • 10,000 subscribers × 2% conversion = 200 patrons
  • 200 patrons × $5/month average pledge = $1,000/month
  • After Patreon fees (~8%) and payment processing (~3%): ~$890/month net

Compare that to AdSense for the same channel: if it generates 40,000 views/month at $3 RPM, that's $120/month. In this scenario, Patreon wins by a significant margin.

The real answer: For most creators under 100K subscribers, Patreon can easily out-earn AdSense — especially in niche or community-driven content. The constraint isn't subscriber count; it's how willing your audience is to pay directly for your work.

Should You Use Both?

Yes — and most successful mid-tier creators do. AdSense requires no extra work once you're monetized. Patreon requires upkeep (exclusive content, community management), but it provides the income stability that AdSense's variability cannot. The combination creates a revenue floor (Patreon) and a revenue ceiling that scales with your growth (AdSense).

The best strategy: launch Patreon once you have 1K–5K engaged subscribers, offer genuine value at $3–$5 tiers, and let AdSense compound as your view count grows.
