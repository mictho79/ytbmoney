Why Niche Matters More Than Subscribers

A finance creator with 50K subscribers can easily out-earn a gaming creator with 500K subscribers. The reason comes down to advertiser demand. Finance companies, insurance providers, and software businesses pay $15–$50 CPM to reach their target audience on YouTube. Gaming advertisers rarely exceed $5 CPM.

This means that a 10x difference in subscribers can be completely wiped out by a 10x difference in RPM. Choosing the right niche — or gradually pivoting toward higher-value content — is often the most impactful decision a creator can make for their revenue.

How to Switch to a Higher-RPM Niche

  • Identify adjacent niches: A gaming channel can add tech review content. A cooking channel can add nutrition or health content. Adjacent pivots retain existing subscribers while attracting higher-CPM advertisers.
  • Create hybrid content: "How I Invested My YouTube Money" from a gaming creator naturally attracts finance advertisers without alienating the gaming audience.
  • Target US, UK, CA audiences: The same niche generates much higher RPM with a Western audience. SEO-optimized English content targeting these markets can double your effective RPM without changing your niche.
  • Enable all ad formats: Skippable, non-skippable, bumper, and overlay ads combined can increase effective RPM by 20–40% over skippable-only setups.
