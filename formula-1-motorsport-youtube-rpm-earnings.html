Why Formula 1 / Motorsport Channels Earn Mid RPM

Premium European + US audience

F1's audience composition is a CPM dream: nearly every viewer is in a tier-1 advertising market. UK and Germany alone represent 24% of viewership, and these countries have some of the highest sports CPMs outside the US.

Luxury advertiser category

F1 sponsors include Rolex, TAG Heuer, Heineken, Crypto.com, Oracle, and AWS. This advertiser mix filters down to YouTube F1 content, producing CPMs 30–60% above generic sports content.

Netflix "Drive to Survive" bump

The series grew US viewership from ~2% to 22% of global F1 audience in five years. This US expansion transformed the economics of F1 YouTube content — channels that would have earned $3 RPM in 2018 now earn $6-8.

FAQ — Formula 1 / Motorsport YouTube Earnings

Why is F1 RPM higher than general soccer?

Audience geography. F1 viewership is concentrated in tier-1 countries (US/UK/Germany/Netherlands/Italy). Soccer viewership is concentrated in tier-3 countries (India, Brazil, Indonesia). The difference in advertiser CPM between these markets is 5–10×.

Can a non-racing driver produce F1 content?

Absolutely. The most successful F1 YouTube channels are typically produced by journalists, engineers, or passionate fans — not drivers. Content quality and consistency matter more than credentials.

Do other motorsports pay as well?

NASCAR and IndyCar RPMs are in the $4–$7 range, slightly below F1. WRC, MotoGP, and Formula E sit around $4–$6. F1 commands the top because of the global premium audience.

How often should F1 channels post?

Most successful F1 channels publish 2–4 videos per race week during the season (March-December) and 1–2 per week off-season. The race calendar dictates content rhythm.
