YouTube RPM by Country

How much YouTube pays per 1,000 views varies dramatically by audience location.

US viewers can be worth 10–20× more than viewers from India or Southeast Asia in terms of YouTube RPM. This guide shows estimated RPM rates for every major region and explains why geography is the biggest single factor in your YouTube earnings.

Why Does YouTube RPM Vary by Country?

YouTube's ad revenue model is driven by advertiser demand. Advertisers pay more to reach audiences with higher purchasing power, greater brand recognition, and higher consumer spending. This creates a large disparity in RPM between countries:

💳

Purchasing Power

Viewers in the US, UK, and Australia have higher disposable income, making them more valuable to advertisers selling products and services.

🏢

Advertiser Competition

More companies compete to advertise to US/EU audiences, driving up CPM via auction. Less competition in developing markets keeps CPMs lower.

🌐

E-commerce Maturity

Mature e-commerce markets (US, UK, DE) have higher ad intent and conversion rates, making impressions more valuable to performance advertisers.
