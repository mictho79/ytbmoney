How Much Does a 10-Minute YouTube Video Earn?

Ten minutes is the magic threshold that unlocks mid-roll ads — here's exactly what that means for your earnings.

🧮

Why 10 Minutes Is a Magic Number on YouTube

YouTube requires a video to be at least 8 minutes long to be eligible for mid-roll ads, but creators widely aim for 10 minutes to give the algorithm enough breathing room to insert at least one mid-roll comfortably. Mid-roll ads (ads that appear during the video rather than before or after) are the highest-value ad placement on the platform because viewers are already engaged when they appear.

Before the 8-minute threshold, a video can only show pre-roll and post-roll ads. Once you cross 10 minutes, YouTube's system typically inserts 1–2 mid-roll placements automatically, boosting your effective RPM by 20–40% compared to a shorter video with the same view count.

The table below shows estimated earnings for a 10-minute video (mid-rolls enabled) at three common RPM tiers. These figures assume mid-roll ads are active and no manual ad placement adjustments were made.

RPM (Revenue Per Mille) = earnings per 1,000 views, after YouTube's 45% cut. Low RPM is typical for gaming/entertainment; high RPM is typical for finance, SaaS, or B2B niches.

How Mid-Roll Ads Boost a 10-Minute Video's Earnings

Without mid-roll ads, a 10-minute video at $5 RPM earning 100K views would earn $500. But RPM figures already reflect the mix of ad types that actually served on your channel. When mid-rolls are enabled for the first time on a channel, creators typically see an RPM increase of 20–40% because each mid-roll impression adds incremental ad revenue on top of the pre-roll baseline.

Key insight: Going from 9 minutes 45 seconds to 10 minutes 15 seconds on a video can meaningfully increase its lifetime revenue — not just by unlocking mid-rolls, but because YouTube's algorithm tends to surface longer videos in "Up Next" recommendations, driving additional organic views.

What Affects Earnings Beyond Video Length?

Video length is just one lever. These factors also determine what a 10-minute video actually earns:

  • Niche / content category — Finance and tech videos command $8–$20 RPM; entertainment and gaming typically see $2–$5 RPM.
  • Audience geography — Views from the US, UK, Australia, and Canada generate 3–5× more ad revenue per view than views from South Asia or Southeast Asia.
  • Seasonality — Q4 (October–December) RPMs are typically 30–60% higher than Q1 due to advertiser holiday spend.
  • Ad blocker prevalence — Channels with younger, tech-savvy audiences may see 15–25% of viewers using ad blockers, reducing monetized view counts.
  • Watch time percentage — Mid-roll ads only fire if viewers watch past the placement point. A video with poor retention may earn less than its length suggests.

How to Maximize Earnings on a 10-Minute Video

To get the most out of the 10-minute threshold:

  1. Hit 10:00–10:30 — Don't pad unnecessarily, but ensure the video clears the threshold comfortably.
  2. Place mid-rolls at natural breaks — Manually position mid-roll ads at chapter transitions or between segments, not mid-sentence, to reduce skip rates and viewer drop-off.
  3. Optimize your thumbnail and title for click-through — More views directly means more ad impressions regardless of RPM.
  4. Focus on high-RPM niches — A 10-minute finance video will always out-earn a 10-minute gaming video at the same view count.
